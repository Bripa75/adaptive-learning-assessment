01

Common questions

Is this a clinical assessment?
No. Ripa Elevate is not a clinical or diagnostic tool and should not replace an evaluation by a licensed professional. It is an educational platform designed to help parents and educators understand a student's functional skill level across key areas. Think of it as a starting point for conversations — not an official diagnosis.
How long does an assessment take?
Each category takes approximately 15–25 minutes, depending on your child's pace. The exam is untimed — there is no pressure to finish quickly. Your child can take breaks between categories.
Can my child do this on their own?
Yes. Once you set up the session, your child can take the assessment independently. The platform includes a read-aloud button on every question, hint support, and step-by-step scaffolding so they can navigate without you hovering. That said, you're welcome to sit with them — especially for the first session.
How soon do I get results?
Results are available immediately when your child submits the assessment. You'll receive an email notification and can view the full report in your parent dashboard right away.
Can I share the report with my child's school?
Absolutely. The PDF report is designed to be shared — clear enough for parents to understand, professional enough for IEP teams. Many parents bring it directly to IEP meetings as supplemental information about their child's real-world skill functioning.
What ages is this designed for?
Ripa Elevate is designed for middle school students — approximately ages 10 to 15. The questions, language, and scenarios are calibrated for this age range, including students with IEPs, autism spectrum conditions, intellectual disabilities, and language processing differences.
