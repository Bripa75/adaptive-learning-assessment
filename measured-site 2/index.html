The Problem

Traditional tests measure the wrong things

Schools have spent decades measuring students against a narrow set of academic benchmarks — right or wrong, above or below grade level. For students who learn differently, this system doesn't just miss the mark. It misses the student entirely.

Measured was built to change that — by two people who watched it fail from inside the system.

The Adaptive Engine

Every student takes their version of the test

The platform calibrates in real time — choosing the next question based on each response, and offering structured support before ever lowering difficulty.

1

Baseline Calibration

A short warm-up phase establishes where the student is across every skill category — without pressure or labels.

2

Real-Time Branching

Each response shapes the next question. The system branches up or down continuously — never following a fixed path.

3

Support Before Reduction

Before lowering difficulty, the platform offers hints, step-by-step breakdowns, and text-to-speech. Independence is preserved first.

4

Skill Snapshot

Results describe what a student can do independently, what they can do with support, and what needs attention — in plain language.

The Level System

Levels, not grades. Growth, not comparison.

Students are not ranked against each other or measured by grade level. Each level describes how independently a student can think and function — with clear, respectful language that educators and families can actually use.

01
Supported
Student demonstrates understanding with structured support, guided steps, or adult assistance throughout.
Approx. Gr. 3–5 functional range
02
Developing
Student can work through problems with occasional hints or prompts — building toward independence.
Approx. Gr. 5–7 functional range
03
Independent
Student navigates most tasks without support, demonstrating reliable independent reasoning and judgment.
Approx. Gr. 7–9 functional range
04
Advanced
Student applies skills flexibly across novel contexts, showing strong reasoning and self-directed thinking.
Gr. 9+ functional range

How Measured works

From the first question to the final report — a complete picture of what the platform does, and why it does it this way.

Step one

The student begins a baseline

No pressure, no stakes. The assessment opens with a brief calibration phase — a small set of questions across each skill category at a neutral difficulty.

5–10 minutes of warm-up questions across math, reading, and life skills
Questions are framed as activities, not a test
The student's pace is respected — no time pressure by default
All accessibility tools (text-to-speech, highlighting) are on from the first question

Step two

The test adapts in real time

Every response shapes the next question. Each skill category branches independently — so a strong reader can still show developing math skills accurately.

Step three

Support before difficulty drops

This is what makes Measured different for higher-functioning students with special needs. The platform never immediately lowers difficulty when a student struggles — it offers structured support first.

Text-to-speech reads the question aloud with word-level highlighting
A hint breaks the problem into a first step, then a second
Step-by-step scaffolding guides reasoning without giving away answers
Only after supports are used does the engine consider adjusting down
The report shows which support was used — a meaningful data point for IEP teams

Our Story

We built this because we watched the current system fail

Measured was created by two people who came at this problem from very different angles — and arrived at exactly the same conclusion.

Licensed Speech Pathologist · Dept. of Education

The clinical co-founder

One co-founder is a Licensed Speech Pathologist with deep experience supporting middle school students with autism, intellectual disabilities, and language processing differences — inside real schools, inside real IEP meetings, inside real classrooms.

Her work inside the Department of Education gave her a front-row seat to something painful: students who were clearly capable of navigating their world — socially, financially, practically — being labeled as "struggling" by assessments that weren't built to see them clearly.

She watched students who couldn't pass a traditional test hold a conversation with grace. Count change accurately. Notice when a friend was upset. Make a good decision under pressure. None of it showed up anywhere.

So she started asking: what would it look like if we assessed what students can actually do?

Platform Builder · Product Strategist

The builder co-founder

The second co-founder came in with a different kind of background — focused on platform development and digital product strategy. What she brought to the table was a belief that the right technology, built thoughtfully, could change the way schools understand students at scale.

When she heard the clinical perspective, the problem became clear: the tools to do something genuinely different existed. What was missing was someone willing to build them the right way — not as a test prep tool, not as a data dashboard, but as a system that actually respected both the student and the educator.

Together, the two founders represent a combination that's rare in education technology: clinical credibility from inside the system, and the product discipline to turn it into something real.

"Every student has more to show than a standardized test will ever capture."

This was the starting point — not an idealistic slogan, but a clinical observation made in real schools over many years.

"Support should come before failure — not after."

The way Measured handles difficulty reduction — offering structure before dropping down — reflects a core pedagogical belief about how students should be treated.

"Life skills aren't a bonus. They're the point."

For many of the students Measured was built for, navigating money, social situations, and daily decisions matters more than passing a math section ever will.
