The Problem

Traditional tests measure the wrong things

Schools have spent decades measuring students against a narrow set of academic benchmarks — right or wrong, above or below grade level. For students who learn differently, this system doesn't just miss the mark. It misses the student entirely.

AdaptIQ was built to change that — by two people who watched it fail from inside the system.

The Adaptive Engine

Every student takes their version of the test

The platform calibrates in real time — choosing the next question based on each response, offering structured support before ever lowering difficulty.

1

Baseline Calibration

A short warm-up phase establishes where the student is across every skill category — without pressure or labels.

2

Real-Time Branching

Each response shapes the next question. The system branches up or down continuously — never following a fixed path.

3

Support Before Reduction

Before lowering difficulty, the platform offers hints, step-by-step breakdowns, and text-to-speech. Independence is preserved first.

4

Skill Snapshot

Results describe what a student can do independently, with support, and what needs attention — in plain language educators can use.

The Level System

Levels, not grades. Growth, not comparison.

Students are not ranked against each other. Each level describes how independently a student can think and function — with respectful language educators and families can act on.

01
Supported
Student demonstrates understanding with structured support, guided steps, or adult assistance throughout.
Approx. Gr. 3–5 functional range
02
Developing
Student can work through problems with occasional hints or prompts — building toward independence.
Approx. Gr. 5–7 functional range
03
Independent
Student navigates most tasks without support, demonstrating reliable independent reasoning and judgment.
Approx. Gr. 7–9 functional range
04
Advanced
Student applies skills flexibly across novel contexts, showing strong reasoning and self-directed thinking.
Gr. 9+ functional range

The Process

How AdaptIQ works

From the first question to the final report — a complete picture of what the platform does, and why it does it this way.

Step one

The student begins a baseline

No pressure, no stakes. The assessment opens with a brief calibration phase — a small set of questions across each skill category at a neutral difficulty.

5–10 minutes of warm-up questions spanning math, reading, life skills, and social reasoning
Questions are framed as activities — not announced as a test
The student sets their own pace — no time pressure by default
All accessibility tools are enabled from the very first question
The engine silently analyzes responses to establish a starting point for adaptive branching

Step two

The test adapts in real time

Every response shapes the next question. Each skill category branches independently — so a strong reader can still show developing math skills accurately.

Correct answers unlock harder questions in that specific category
Incorrect answers trigger a support sequence before any difficulty adjustment
Students never see difficulty labels — the experience feels seamless
No two students take the same test — paths diverge immediately after calibration
The engine uses a modified Item Response Theory model tuned for this age and population

Step three

Support before difficulty drops

The platform never immediately lowers difficulty when a student struggles — it offers structured support first, preserving the student's potential.

Text-to-speech reads the full question aloud with word-level highlighting
A hint breaks the problem into a first step, then a second — never giving the answer
Step-by-step scaffolding guides reasoning without replacing the student's thinking
Only after all supports are offered does the engine consider adjusting difficulty
Support usage becomes meaningful data in the final IEP-aligned report

Our Story

We built this because we watched the current system fail

AdaptIQ was created by two people who came at this problem from very different angles — and arrived at exactly the same conclusion.

Licensed Speech Pathologist · Dept. of Education

The clinical co-founder

One co-founder is a Licensed Speech Pathologist with deep experience supporting middle school students with autism, intellectual disabilities, and language processing differences — inside real schools, inside real IEP meetings, inside real classrooms.

Her work inside the Department of Education gave her a front-row seat to something painful: students who were clearly capable of navigating their world — socially, financially, practically — being labeled as "struggling" by assessments that weren't built to see them clearly.

She watched students who couldn't pass a traditional test hold a conversation with grace. Count change accurately. Notice when a friend was upset. Make a good decision under pressure. None of it showed up anywhere in the data. So she started asking: what would it look like if we assessed what students can actually do?

Platform Builder · Product Strategist

The builder co-founder

The second co-founder came in with a different background — focused on platform development and digital product strategy. What she brought to the table was a belief that the right technology, built thoughtfully, could change the way schools understand students at scale.

When she heard the clinical perspective, the problem became clear: the tools to do something genuinely different already existed. What was missing was someone willing to build them the right way — not as a test prep tool, not as a data dashboard for its own sake, but as a system that actually respected both the student and the educator.

Together, the two founders represent a rare combination: clinical credibility from inside the system, and the product discipline to turn it into something real.

Frequently Asked
Is AdaptIQ available for schools now?
We're currently in an early-access phase, working directly with a small number of schools and IEP teams. Reach out and we'll let you know what's available and when broader access opens.
What grades does this work for?
AdaptIQ is designed primarily for middle school students — grades 6–8 — including higher-functioning students in special education settings. The level system (L1–L4) replaces grade-based framing entirely.
Can results be used in IEP documentation?
Yes. The dashboard is designed with IEP teams in mind — showing skill-by-skill levels, support used, and specific recommendations that map to IEP goal language. Reports are exportable and written to be directly useful in team meetings.
Does it work for students with autism or ID?
Yes — AdaptIQ was specifically designed with higher-functioning students with autism, intellectual disabilities, and language processing differences in mind. The accessibility tools, scenario-based questions, and level system all reflect that population.
Is there a cost to pilot?
We're offering free pilots to a small number of schools during our early-access phase. Reach out and we'll talk through what makes sense for your situation.
